# 🏦 AML Fraud Detection & Analytics

> **End-to-end Data Analyst portfolio project** — ETL pipeline, SQL analysis, Python analytics, and Excel reporting on a synthetic Anti-Money Laundering (AML) and fraud detection dataset.

---

## 📌 Project Overview

Financial institutions process millions of transactions daily, making it critical to detect fraudulent activity and comply with Anti-Money Laundering (AML) regulations. This project simulates a real-world data analyst workflow at a banking institution, covering:

- Synthetic transaction data generation (20,000 transactions, 500 customers)
- A production-style **ETL pipeline** with data validation and feature engineering
- **SQL-based analysis** queries for fraud detection, structuring detection, and risk profiling
- **Python visualizations** across 8 analytical dimensions
- A formatted **Excel stakeholder report** with 6 analytical sheets

## 🔍 Key Analyses

### 1. Fraud Rate by Transaction Type
Identifies which transaction types carry the highest fraud risk — Wire Transfers and Cash Deposits show significantly elevated fraud rates, consistent with real-world AML patterns.

### 2. Monthly Fraud Trend
Dual-axis chart overlaying total transaction volume with monthly fraud rate to detect seasonal patterns and anomalies.

### 3. Country Risk Analysis
Ranks countries by fraud rate. High-risk jurisdictions (NG, RU, AE) exhibit fraud rates 2–3× the global average, triggering enhanced due diligence flags.

### 4. Structuring Detection
Detects potential structuring behavior — transactions deliberately kept just below the $10,000 BSA reporting threshold — a key AML red flag.

### 5. Fraud by Hour of Day
Identifies that transactions occurring between 11pm–6am have significantly higher fraud rates, enabling time-based risk scoring rules.

### 6. Customer Risk Profiling
Segments customers into Low / Medium / High risk tiers based on composite scoring, cross-referenced with KYC status and PEP designation.

### 7. Merchant Category Risk
Highlights Crypto Exchange and Money Transfer merchants as the highest-risk categories for fraud exposure.

### 8. Amount Distribution
Log-scale comparison of legitimate vs. fraudulent transaction amounts, revealing distinct distributional differences useful for anomaly thresholds.

## 💡 Key Findings

- **Overall fraud rate: ~5.1%** — elevated vs. industry baseline, reflecting high-risk transaction mix
- **Wire Transfers and Cash Deposits** carry the highest fraud rates among transaction types
- **Structuring patterns** detected across multiple customers with repeated transactions in the $9,000–$9,999 range
- **KYC-unverified customers** are 2.4× more likely to be associated with fraudulent activity
- **Politically Exposed Persons (PEPs)** account for a disproportionate share of high-risk alerts
- **Late-night transactions (11pm–6am)** show measurably higher fraud rates
